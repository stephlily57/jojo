## THE HUNTING FRAMEWORK

### 1. REAL SOLVABILITY DETECTION

**The Problem**: Not all opportunities are real.
- 40% look good but have hidden problems
- 30% are manipulated presentations
- 20% are actually struggling
- Only ~10% are genuinely solvable

**The Solution**: 10-Marker Analysis

```
┌─ Cash Flow (25%) ─────┐
│  • Monthly positive?  │
│  • Runway length?     │ Eliminate non-viable
│  • Burn rate trend?   │
└───────────────────────┘
         ↓
┌─ Recurring Revenue (20%) ────┐
│  • % of total revenue?        │
│  • Customer churn rate?       │ Identify stability
│  • Predictability score?      │
└───────────────────────────────┘
         ↓
┌─ [7 More Markers] ────────────┐
│  Assets, Loyalty, Position,   │
│  Growth, Commitment, IP,      │ Complete picture
│  Leverage                      │
└───────────────────────────────┘
```

**Result**: 
- Real viable businesses identified quickly
- Problems flagged with specific reasons
- Investment thesis documented
- Risk assessment clear

### 2. FIVE DISCRETION LEVELS

How visible is each opportunity?

```
LEVEL 5: EVIDENT (85-100 score)
├─ Obvious to market
├─ Public data confirms
├─ Multiple players interested
└─ Example: Profitable SaaS, +25% growth, VC-grade

LEVEL 4: DISCRETION_LEVEL_1 (70-84 score)
├─ Good signals, not shouting
├─ Requires industry knowledge
├─ Selective approach works
└─ Example: Stable agency, +15% growth, good margins

LEVEL 3: DISCRETION_LEVEL_2 (55-69 score)
├─ Hidden in plain sight
├─ Requires competitive research
├─ Niche knowledge valuable
└─ Example: E-commerce niche, patient capital needed

LEVEL 2: HIDDEN (40-54 score)
├─ Invisible unless searching
├─ Requires network activation
├─ Rarely exposed to market
└─ Example: Profitable consulting, low public profile

LEVEL 1: SECRET (<40 score)
├─ Ultra-rare, premium discovery
├─ Requires elite network access
├─ Exceptional opportunities
└─ Example: Network-effect platform, 50%+ growth hidden
```

**Business Implication**:
- EVIDENT → Competitive market, fast execution
- DISCRETION_LEVEL_1 → Balanced, good options
- DISCRETION_LEVEL_2 → Expert advantage, patience pays
- HIDDEN → Network-dependent, relationship-based
- SECRET → Elite deals, premium returns

## CASH HORIZONS & DEPLOYMENT STRATEGIES

### THE FIVE TIMELINES

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  TIMELINE          HORIZON    CAPITAL SOURCE   RETURNS  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  24h = URGENT      Today      Unpaid invoices  1.1-1.2x │
│  48h = QUICK       2 Days     Early payments   1.2-1.3x │
│  72h = RAPID       3 Days     Monthly revenue  1.3-1.5x │
│  6m = GROWTH       180 Days   Recurring base   2-4x     │
│  2y = STRATEGIC    730 Days   Valuation       10-25x    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### USE CASES BY TIMELINE

**24h URGENT CASH**
- When: Business needs immediate capital
- What: Payroll due, supplier payment, tax debt
- How: Inject cash, take % revenue share starting next month
- Return: Quick (1.1-1.2x) because risk is lower
- Size: $5K-$50K typical

**48h QUICK DEPLOYMENT**
- When: Working capital needs (inventory, receivables)
- What: Cash to stabilize operations
- How: Structure with milestones (30-60 days)
- Return: Medium-quick (1.2-1.3x)
- Size: $10K-$100K typical

**72h RAPID DEPLOYMENT**
- When: Tactical needs (hiring, marketing spike)
- What: Access to next month's revenue
- How: Revenue share starts day 1
- Return: 1.3-1.5x over 3-6 months
- Size: $20K-$200K typical

**6 MONTH GROWTH**
- When: Opportunity to scale (market expansion, hiring)
- What: Capital for growth investing
- How: Provide funding, track KPIs, share upside
- Return: 2-4x (if growth happens, even more)
- Size: $50K-$500K typical

**2 YEAR STRATEGIC**
- When: Category dominance opportunity
- What: Transform business through acquisition, consolidation, or category play
- How: Partner deeply, share equity/profits
- Return: 10-25x (if category thesis works)
- Size: $500K-$10M typical

## NATURAL OFFER GENERATION

### The Problem with Forced Offers

❌ "Here's our standard deal"
❌ "Take it or leave it"
❌ "One size fits all"
❌ Misaligned incentives
❌ Low acceptance rates

### The Natural Offer Approach

✅ **REVENUE ACCELERATION** (For high-growth companies)
- "You're growing 25% but limited by capital"
- "We provide $X for hiring / market expansion"
- "We take 20% of NEW revenue only"
- "Incentive: Both want growth"
- Acceptance: 60%+

✅ **QUICK CASH INJECTION** (For positive-cash companies)
- "You're profitable but working capital limited"
- "We provide $X for 3 months"
- "Repaid from operating cash flow"
- "Incentive: You get financial breathing room"
- Acceptance: 65%+

✅ **STRATEGIC ACQUISITION** (For mature companies)
- "You've built something valuable"
- "We offer $X to acquire at 4x revenue"
- "You could exit, or grow together"
- "Incentive: Founder gets liquidity or upside"
- Acceptance: 35%+ (many choose not to sell)

✅ **REVENUE SHARING PARTNERSHIP** (For well-aligned businesses)
- "We both want mutual growth"
- "Share 25% of revenue increases"
- "No capital from us, partnership model"
- "Incentive: Aligned on same goal"
- Acceptance: 45%+

✅ **ASSET PURCHASE** (For asset-heavy businesses)
- "Your technology/customer list has independent value"
- "We acquire that asset at 2-3x value"
- "Keep operations, sell specific asset"
- "Incentive: Creator capital without full exit"
- Acceptance: 40%+

✅ **GROWTH INVESTMENT** (For scaling companies)
- "You're profitable and growing 20%+"
- "We take 12% equity for $X"
- "Build together for 5-10 years"
- "Incentive: Become leaders in category"
- Acceptance: 30%+ (longer timeline, bigger vision)

- Direct approach
- "We see what you've built"
- Expect: Fast conversation, clear interest
- Timeline: 1-2 weeks to term sheet

**DISCRETION_LEVEL_1**:
- Consultative approach
- "We know your market deeply"
- Expect: Warmer response than cold call
- Timeline: 2-3 weeks to discussion

**DISCRETION_LEVEL_2**:
- Research-backed approach
- "We found something interesting in niche X"

- Timeline: 3-4 weeks to meeting

**HIDDEN**:
- Referral/network introduction
- "Someone we trust told us about you"
- Expect: Higher credibility
- Timeline: 1-2 weeks after introduction

**SECRET**:
- Elite network activation
- Premium introduction, in-person meeting
- Expect: Highly receptive (word is in)

### PHASE 3: OFFER GENERATION (Week 2-4)

**Actions**:
1. Present right offer for stage
2. Customize with specific numbers
3. Show mutual alignment
4. Build investment thesis (why good for both)
5. Move to term sheet

**Offer Selection by Situation**:
- Struggling with cash? → Quick Cash Injection
- Growing but capital-limited? → Revenue Acceleration
- Mature and profitable? → Strategic Acquisition or Revenue Share
- Platform with network effects? → Strategic Equity

### PHASE 4: EXECUTION (Week 3-6)

**Milestones**:
1. Term sheet signed (Week 1)
2. Due diligence (Week 2-3, parallel)
3. Documentation (Week 3-4, parallel)
4. Close & funding (Week 4)
